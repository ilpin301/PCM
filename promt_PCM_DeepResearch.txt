RESEARCH QUESTION
Find and analyze the most authoritative scientific literature on Phase Change
Materials (PCM), covering BOTH meanings of the term: (A) chalcogenide
phase-change materials for memory, electronics and photonics (Ge-Sb-Te / GST,
GeTe, Sb2Te3, doped and interfacial PCM, GeTe/Sb2Te3 superlattices, PCRAM,
optical data storage, neuromorphic and photonic computing) and (B) phase-change
materials for thermal energy storage and thermal management (paraffins, fatty
acids, sugar alcohols, salt hydrates, eutectics, microencapsulated /
shape-stabilized / composite PCMs; buildings, electronics cooling, solar
thermal). Map the materials, mechanisms, characterization methods, recent
advances, applications and open challenges across both domains, and note where
the two fields connect.

WHICH SOURCES TO FIND (and how to rank them)
- Only scientific, peer-reviewed, authoritative sources: reputable journals and
  high-impact reviews from Nature / Nature Materials / Nature Communications,
  Science / AAAS, Wiley (Advanced Materials, Advanced Functional Materials),
  Elsevier, ACS, RSC, IEEE, AIP (Applied Physics Letters, J. Appl. Phys.), IOP,
  Springer. Treat arXiv preprints as supporting material only and mark them as
  non-peer-reviewed. Exclude blogs, vendor/marketing pages, news aggregators and
  encyclopedias (e.g. Wikipedia) as primary evidence.
- Language preference: English first, then German, then Russian. Include German
  or Russian sources when they add value not well covered in English, and
  summarize them in English.
- Recency: no hard date cutoff, but prefer the most recent work when sources are
  otherwise comparable; still include seminal/foundational works for context.
- PRIORITY: give extra weight to sources that explicitly use and describe
  Atomic Force Microscopy and Scanning Electron Microscopy characterization,
  especially those containing the phrases "Atom Force Microscopy methods"
  (a.k.a. "Atomic Force Microscopy", AFM) and "Scanning Electron Microscopy
  methods" (a.k.a. SEM). Treat AFM/SEM characterization of morphology,
  microstructure, surface topography and phase/grain evolution as a central
  thread of the research.
